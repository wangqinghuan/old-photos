These graves are in Roermond. I remember visiting them when I was in high school. This left a big impression on me at the time. The graves are called "Grave with the hands". The Wikipedia page can be found here: https://nl.wikipedia.org/wiki/Graf_met_de_handjes. Translation/summary of Dutch Wikipedia page using AI: The Wikipedia text for "Graf met de handjes" (Grave with the Hands) can be translated and summarized as follows: The "Grave with the Hands" (Graf met de handjes) is a monumental tombstone located in Roermond, Netherlands, at the Oude Kerkhof (Old Cemetery). It marks the graves of Josephina van Aefferden, a Catholic, and Jacobus van Gorkum, a Protestant. They were married in life but had to be buried in separate sections of the cemetery due to their religious differences. The monument consists of two identical neo-Gothic grave markers, placed on a limestone base, made of sandstone columns with a gable-shaped top. At the top of each marker is a cross. On his side is the coat of arms of the van Gorkum family, and on her side is her arms of alliance. A wrought-iron fence surrounds the base of each. The most distinctive feature is the depiction of two hands: a man's hand from his monument clasping a woman's hand from hers, reaching across the wall that separates their graves. This symbolizes the connection between the spouses across the boundaries of religion, family alliances, and even death. The motto "Vivit post funera virtus" (Virtue outlives death), originally from the van Gorkum family coat of arms, is chiseled on both monuments. The monument was likely made in 1888, the year of Josephina's death, possibly by the Roermond firm Atelier Cuypers-Stoltzenberg. It was listed as a rijksmonument (state monument) in 2002. The "Grave with the Hands" is a powerful testament to their love and the triumph of personal devotion over rigid societal constraints.
