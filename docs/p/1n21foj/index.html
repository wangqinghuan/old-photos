Strange_Shadows-4554 赞2025/8/28
A lobotomy severed connections in the prefrontal cortex. They knew that the prefrontal cortex regulated emotions and social behavior, so the idea was that an operation in that area would help alleviate issues regarding that. It was popularized in the World War II era because asylums were becoming overcrowded and there was an increase in cases of severe depression and PTSD. Mental illness wasn’t well understood and it was pushed as a procedure that could people who were mentally unwell better readjust if they weren’t responding to other treatments. But in practice, they simply didn’t know enough about brain activity and essential function and they did become more emotionally apathetic but at the cost of a loss of their personality, self control and cognitive function. In the grand scheme of things, lobotomy was more a fad than anything else, it was already getting pushed out in the late 50s as non-surgical interventions (anti-psychotic medications being one) came out and the media praise turned to hostility fairly quickly after those who underwent the procedures suffered devastating consequences that didn’t go away after a few years.
